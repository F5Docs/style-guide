# Please and Thank You

## Guidance

Do not use "please" or "thank you" in technical documentation. Neither word changes what the reader needs to do, and both slow down a reader who's scanning for the next step. Technical docs are reference material, not correspondence — the reader isn't doing the writer a favor by following instructions.

## Examples

**Do:**
> Enter your license key to activate the product.

> Restart the service after you apply the change.

**Don't:**
> Please enter your license key to activate the product.

> Thank you for your patience. Please restart the service after you
> apply the change.

## Notes

This rule applies to procedural, conceptual, and reference content without exception.

### Exception: error messages

[Writing error messages](../error-messages/writing-error-messages.md) permits "please" in one specific case: when the error is caused by F5 software, not the user. There, "please" softens a message about a problem the user did nothing to cause.

> The network connection was lost. Please reenter your password.

Outside that one case, don't use "please" in error messages either.

"Thank you" has no exception. Don't use it anywhere in technical content, including error messages.
