# Dates and times

Use consistent, unambiguous date and time formats. Global readers and machine translation both depend on it.

## Dates

Write dates as Month Day, Year. Spell out the month. Don't use ordinal suffixes.

| Use | Not |
|---|---|
| June 17, 2026 | 17 June 2026 |
| June 17, 2026 | June 17th, 2026 |
| June 17, 2026 | 6/17/2026 |

For a date range within one month, don't repeat the month: June 17-19, 2026.

For a date range across months, repeat both month and day: June 28-July 2, 2026.

## Times

Write times with a space before a.m. or p.m., lowercase, with periods. Always include a timezone abbreviation.

| Use | Not |
|---|---|
| 10 a.m. PT | 10AM |
| 10 a.m. PT | 10:00 AM |
| 2 p.m. PT | 2:00 p.m. PT |

Omit ":00" for on-the-hour times. Use noon or midnight instead of 12 p.m. or 12 a.m. -- readers routinely misread which is which.

Spell out the timezone on first use, then use the abbreviation: Pacific Time (PT), Eastern Time (ET).

## Availability

Use 24x7 for continuous availability. Don't use 24/7 or "round the clock."
